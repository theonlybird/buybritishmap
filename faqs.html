Frequently asked questions

What do the Gold and Silver badges mean?

Gold means fully made or grown in the UK, including the main material. Silver means genuinely made in the UK but with an imported main material or some imported stock. Products merely designed, branded, printed or packed in the UK don’t qualify at all.

Why is a famous British brand only Silver?

Because the tier follows the material, not the reputation. Northampton’s great shoemakers are Silver because the calf leather is imported — superb British making, imported main material. The badge is a statement of fact, not a criticism.

Do businesses pay to be listed?

No. Nobody pays for a listing, a badge, or placement — and nobody can. Tiers are decided by evidence alone, and every listing shows its evidence publicly.

How do businesses get on the map?

We research and verify each listing against public evidence — the business’s own website, its stated materials and making locations. Anyone can suggest a business; every suggestion is checked against the standard before it appears.

Something on the map is wrong — how do I report it?

Please contact us with the listing name and what’s wrong. If a business believes its tier understates it — for example, we couldn’t evidence where its clay or fabric comes from — we’ll gladly review with better evidence.

Why are Northern Ireland and the Isle of Man included?

Northern Ireland is part of the UK. The Isle of Man is a Crown dependency rather than part of the UK — we include it under a broader British Isles view, and we say so openly (it’s home to some of the finest making in these islands).

Why isn’t my favourite maker listed?

Probably because we haven’t got to them yet — the map grows in researched batches. Tell us about them and we’ll check them out.

Is this sponsored or affiliated with anyone?

No. Buy British is independent, carries no advertising, takes no commission, and has no connection to any retailer or trade body.
